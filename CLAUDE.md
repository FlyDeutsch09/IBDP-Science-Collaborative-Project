# PROJECT CONTEXT

## Official Topic

Acoustic Metamaterials:
Sustainable futuristic alternatives to conventional architecture and sound design.

---

## Real-World Problem

Noise pollution is a major global issue.

Examples include:

* Traffic noise
* HVAC systems
* Construction activity
* Urban infrastructure

Consequences include:

* Reduced productivity
* Sleep disruption
* Stress
* Cardiovascular health impacts

Conventional acoustic foam:

* Often petroleum-based
* Difficult to recycle
* Requires large thicknesses for low-frequency absorption

Acoustic metamaterials offer an alternative because they manipulate sound through geometry rather than material mass alone.

---

# PROJECT VISION

The team will design, build and test:

EcoMetaPanel

A sustainable acoustic metamaterial wall panel made primarily from recycled and/or bio-based materials.

The panel should demonstrate improved low-frequency sound absorption compared with a conventional flat panel of the same thickness.

---

# FINAL RESEARCH QUESTION

To what extent can a recycled-material acoustic metamaterial panel reduce low-frequency noise compared with conventional acoustic panels of the same thickness?
